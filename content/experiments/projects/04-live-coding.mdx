After my formal studies to become a filmmaker, I got really interested in live processes for editing and manipulating video, which led me to explore more about VJing and other live video practices.
Nowadays, audiovisual performances are a big part of my artistic practice and I really enjoy being involved in building the aesthetics, atmosphere and narratives for that specific time of interaction with the public.
My VJ practice is a live exploration of visual content and music, often performed alongside artist and partner Francisco Barretto. My typical workflow involves a hybrid setup using Resolume and Hydra to mix pre-rendered video with live-coded shaders in real-time. For every performance, I coordinate with the audio to build a specific atmosphere, aligning visual narratives with the sonic storytelling of the set.

We have performed multiple live sets at Wonderville in Brooklyn and have been featured twice in LiveCodeNYC events, including a showcase at Harvestworks. These performances allow me to test modular glitching and feedback loops in a high-pressure live environment. Each set is an experiment in real-time interaction, where I manipulate visual textures to react dynamically to the rhythm and energy of the space.
