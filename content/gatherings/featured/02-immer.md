IMMER, also known as IMMERSPHERE, celebrates large-scale digital experiences of art and technology. The festival has had three successful editions hosted in Brazil, with a focus on the power of dome displays as a medium for immersive storytelling. As the first and only initiative of its kind in the country, IMMER serves as a global gathering point for artists pushing the boundaries of spatial media.

Built on a conceptual tripod of art – science – education, the transdisciplinary initiative showcases recent fulldome productions and provides a platform to foster discussions around the art form, its production chain, and the future of immersive art — through concurrent workshops, academic presentations, lectures and roundtables.

Over the years, the festival has received support through government grants to host entirely free sessions, presenting programs of pre-rendered fulldome films alongside live performances and interactive elements engaging the audience. One of its greatest strengths stems from its strategic location, which empowers local artists through educational activities, boosts local fulldome production, and features a curatorial approach that fosters greater cultural diversity within its program.

As Lead Producer since 2021, I lead the complex logistics and technical coordination required to bring world-class immersive art to life. My responsibilities span the entire production lifecycle — from managing relationships with international artists to supervising the installation of high-end projection systems, managing technical teams, vendor workflows, and securing grants and investments. By balancing executive production with a deep understanding of immersive design, I ensure that each edition delivers a robust, high-fidelity experience for both creators and the public.
