## Overview

<Figure src="/projects/buriti/assets/buriti-main.jpeg" aspect="4/3" />

Buriti is an audiovisual ode to the native roots and indigenous wisdom of South America, specifically drawing inspiration from the Tupi-Guarani word for "the tree of life." The work explores the intrinsic connection between humans and the environment, manifesting through a visual journey across the four fundamental elements: air, earth, fire, and water.

<Figure src="/projects/buriti/assets/buriti-01.png" aspect="3/1" />

Developed for the competitive screening at the Mapping do Castelo Festival in Bahia, Brazil, the piece functions as a digital ritual of remembrance. It celebrates the knowledge of original populations and recognizes their essential role in the growth and preservation of our collective ecosystem.

<Figure src="/projects/buriti/assets/buriti-02.png" aspect="3/1" />

## Process

The visual language of the piece was crafted using text-to-image AI techniques, allowing for a dreamlike interpretation of South American palm forests and elemental transformations. By feeding the AI conceptual prompts rooted in indigenous cosmologies, the project creates a bridge between ancient oral traditions and modern machine learning.

The projection mapping process scales these intimate, generated textures to the monumental architecture of a castle, transforming the physical stone into a living canvas of cultural heritage.
