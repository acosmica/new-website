## Overview

thecode studio was commissioned by Coca-Cola in partnership with a art and technology event in Salvador, Brazil to build an innovative Christmas activation. This installation sought to turn an urban landmark into a space of celebration and community connection. The project utilized a low-resolution LED curtain covering a massive building facade, reimagining the structure as a living digital canvas. Designed to engage families and tourists alike, the display moved away from traditional broadcasting and toward a responsive, human-centric experience. By capturing the presence of those passing by, the installation turned the public square into a stage, inviting the residents of Salvador to see themselves reflected in the holiday lights of their own city.

## Process

The piece relied on high-quality computer vision technology to track the silhouettes. As people walked or danced near the camera station, their body shapes were abstracted into vibrant light patterns that mimicked their every move across the LED panel. The low-resolution nature of the LED curtain was an aesthetic choice, turning sharp human movements into soft, glowing impressions that felt like a digital manifestation of the holiday spirit. I co-directed the creative vision and managed the production from initial concept to final installation, performing in-depth research about softwares and the workflow needed for this large-scale project. At thecode, we spent significant time testing movement-tracking algorithms that could distinguish individual participants within a crowded public space, ensuring the interaction felt personal despite its monumental scale. This required a seamless integration of physical computing and real-time visual processing, resulting in a robust system capable of handling the high-traffic environment.
