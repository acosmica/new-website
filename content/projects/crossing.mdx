## Overview

"The Crossing" (Travessia, in Portuguese) follows Alice, a young woman who, during a run through a forest, discovers a portal marked on a tree and is transported to a parallel dimension. In this space, occupying the same location but in a different time, she encounters other women and is challenged to complete a purposeful crossing. The project serves as a cinematic reflection on the reconnection with ancestral wisdom and the power of female existence. It intentionally confronts socio-political themes, including the violence of the patriarchy and the historical persecution of women and minorities, positioning collective resistance as a vital tool for healing and survival.

During the film, scattered memories and experiences are put together in front of the spectator, inviting them to embody Alice’s experience of confusion, conflict and transformation.

## Process

Initially developed as an interactive art piece for portable devices, the work utilized the smartphones hardware's internal sensors to drive the narrative. The interactor’s physical movement of the device allowed them to navigate between different points of view and parallel storylines, ultimately leading to various possible endings. This technical choice mirrors the fragmented nature of memory and historical archives, here translated to the fragmented reality of Alice that is, at the same time, experiencing multiple existences.

<Figure src="/projects/crossing/assets/crossing-02.jpg" aspect="3/2" />

In 2026, a cinematic short film edit was also produced while still highlighting the multi-linearity and the multiplicity of these final destinations. The project is the culmination of years of research into the myths of witches and the invisible history of persecuted women, translating scholarly archival excavation into a sensory cinematic language.

<Pair a="/projects/crossing/assets/crossing-04.jpg" b="/projects/crossing/assets/crossing-05.jpg" aspect="2/3" />

The film was created as my final thesis for the Bachelor of Cinema and Audiovisual at UFPB (Brazil) and was the result of an extensive collective effort involving fellow film students, local actresses, and partner institutions. The development process required balancing traditional narrative filmmaking with the logic of interaction design, ensuring that the transitions between points of view felt seamless and narratively justified. We focused on the symbolic weight of the portal and the forest environment, using these as metaphors for the dialogue between the contemporary world and an ancestral realm of knowledge. This project laid the research foundation for a larger body of work investigating the intersections of magic, technology, and memory.
