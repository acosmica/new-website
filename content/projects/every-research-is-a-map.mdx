## Overview

Commissioned by the renowned cultural institution Itaú Cultural, Toda Pesquisa é um Mapa (Every Research is a Map) was developed in a close, collaborative partnership with their MediaLab. The project serves as a digital centerpiece for the Ocupação Ana Mae, an exhibition honoring Ana Mae Barbosa, the pioneer of art education in Brazil. The installation aims to translate her profound intellectual impact into a spatial experience. By mapping decades of academic writings and citations, the project visualizes the vast universe of her influence, allowing users to explore how her theories have branched out into thousands of interconnected clusters of thought across the Brazilian and international educational landscape.

<Figure src="/projects/every-research/assets/pesquisa-01.png" aspect="16/9" />

The experience is powered by a sophisticated graph system and generative AI that processes massive datasets of academic publications. Each "star" in the 3D galaxy represents an individual article, while clusters represent thematic similarities or shared ideological lineages. This creates an intuitive, exploratory tool where users can traverse the 3D environment to see how ideas interact, merge, and evolve. The project treats scholarship as a living organism; it moves away from the linearity of a bibliography and toward the expansiveness of an atlas, framing the act of research as a journey through a luminous, interconnected map of collective memory.

## Process

As a Creative Technologist for thecode, I served as the project manager and a contributor to the web development and UX design. Working alongside Itaú Cultural's MediaLab, our team of specialists at thecode defined the interaction logic that allows users to move seamlessly through high-density data without losing their sense of orientation. From defining the visual identity and colors and refining the 3D navigation to overseeing the implementation of the graph-based AI, I ensured the final product balanced high-performance engineering with a visionary, intentional tribute to accessible education and South-American cultural history.
