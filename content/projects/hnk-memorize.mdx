## Overview

This brand activation transforms the Heineken star into a playful challenge designed to engage market visitors. By turning the iconic brand symbol into a functional gaming interface inspired by the classic Simon, the piece encourages brand recall. The installation challenges players to replicate increasingly complex sequences of light and sound, maintaining a digital score and providing a tactile, multi-sensory experience.

<Figure src="/projects/memorize/assets/memorize-01.png" aspect="16/9" />

## Process

The star is a custom-fabricated panel completely equipped and programmed by the studio thecode with integrated capacitive touch sensors, allowing the entire surface of specific points to act as responsive buttons without the need for mechanical switches. The core of the piece is a microcontroller programmed to manage the game’s logic: triggering rhythmic blinks via built-in LEDs, outputting synchronized audio cues, and tracking player progress in real time. The interaction flow is designed to be intuitive yet progressively difficult, utilizing the capacitive sensors to ensure a seamless and modern user interface.

<Figure src="/projects/memorize/assets/memorize-03.png" aspect="4/3" />

As an Interaction Designer and Project Manager for thecode, I collaborated on the electronics planning and the assembly of the physical computing system. I did supervision of the project implementation from concept to assembly and maintenance, delivering around 10 stars to different regions of the country. Additionally, I was also responsible for the motion graphics of the content on the TV, with videos keeping the score of the player and providing instructions.
