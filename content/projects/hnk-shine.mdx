## Overview

Partnering with TIWA Creative agency, this project involved the creation of a personalized interactive display for Heineken, utilizing the brand’s signature star geometry as a functional light sculpture. The star totem serves as a responsive landmark within a space, moving beyond static signage to become an active invitation in the brand environment. By integrating a presence sensor, the panel acknowledges the movement of nearby visitors, transforming passive observation into a moment of direct engagement.

<Figure src="/projects/shine/assets/shine-02.png" aspect="3/2" />

## Process

The star is constructed from over a hundred individually soldered addressable LEDs, allowing for granular control over color and patterns. A microcontroller housed within the totem acts as the central processor, interfacing with a proximity sensor to detect audience movement. These physical triggers are mapped to specific animation changes, enabling the star to pulse, shift, or accelerate its lighting sequences in real time based on visitor flow.

As part of the team at thecode, I collaborated on the electronics assembling and project management for the experiential side of the project, managing the whole life cycle of the project with needed maintenance.
