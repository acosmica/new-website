## Overview

This installation was designed as a centerpiece for a Heineken branding space in a popular supermarket, focusing on the immersive potential of light and motion. The core of the experience is a custom brand stand featuring the brand’s signature star as a central cutout. By integrating a presence sensor into the architecture, the space becomes aware of its visitors. As they move through the stand, the environment reacts instantly, triggering shifting visual patterns that wrap around the physical geometry of the star. It creates a dynamic promotional area for brand activation that rewards the movement of the crowd.

## Process

The project utilizes high-precision projection mapping to align digital content with the specific contours of the star-shaped cutout. A microcontroller-linked presence sensor serves as the primary input, sending triggers to a media server whenever movement is detected. These triggers initiate real-time transitions between different states of motion graphics, ensuring the visuals feel tethered to the physical presence of the audience.

As an Interactive Designer for thecode, I was responsible for the creative direction of the visual narrative and co-directed the technical design of the interaction flow. I produced all the video content and motion graphics, specifically tailoring the animations to complement the star’s sharp, geometric lines, also defining the timing of how the visuals reacted to visitors to ensure the interaction felt intuitive, fluid, and high-impact.
