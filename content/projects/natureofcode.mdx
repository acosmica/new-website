## Overview

Invited by Daniel Shiffman to perform for the official relaunch event of The Nature of Code, this performance investigates the friction and fusion between organic biological patterns and digital infrastructure. The live piece is a visual inquiry into the very systems Shiffman's work describes (growth, feedback, and complexity) translating these concepts into a audiovisual live coding experience. By manipulating code in real time to alter AI generated videos of nature before a live audience, the performance transforms the clinical precision of algorithms into a fluid, unpredictable landscapes.

## Process

The work mostly happens on Hydra, a browser-based live-coding video synthesizer. The visual pipeline begins with AI-generated video source material, which is then fed into a custom feedback loop. Through real-time coding, I apply modular glitching, distortions, and pixel manipulation, ensuring the visual output evolves in direct dialogue with the sonic environment. Poetically, the piece explores the organic vs. digital dichotomy; the AI loops I create provide the nature with textures and patterns, while the live code acts as the environment, distorting and shaping the growth of the imagery into visuals that feel both hyper-digital and deeply biological.
