## Overview

Portals is an investigation into the sculptural possibilities of light, utilizing holographic fan technology to suspend digital forms in mid-air. The work strips away complexity in favor of a stark, monochromatic aesthetic, using basic geometric shapes to explore the nature of optical illusions.

By creating a focal point where light appears to gain volume and mass, the installation functions as a threshold evoking a portal, where the viewer's physical reality meets a weightless, three-dimensional digital void. It is a playful yet deep exploration of how we perceive depth and matter in an increasingly virtualized world.

## Process

The installation integrates weightless holographic fans with a custom gesture sensor, built specifically for this piece, to create a responsive magical experience. As participants move their hands in front of the sensor, a gesture-tracking technology detects specific interactions, translating these physical inputs into the rotation, scaling, and transformation of the holographic shapes.

Poetically, the piece suggests that the digital world is not a separate destination, but a layer of our own reality that reacts to our touch, inviting a tactile dialogue with the intangible. The creation of the artwork centered on the technical challenge of rendering real-time, three-dimensional assets that could be accurately translated onto a persistent-vision display.
