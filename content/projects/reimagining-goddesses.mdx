## Overview

Reimagining Goddesses serves as a digital inquiry into whether artificial minds can grasp the weight of the sacred. In a contemporary world where the feminine figure is often obscured in the context of divinity, this project uses generative technology to unearth and re-center the woman-as-god. By prompting first-generation Stable Diffusion models with ancestral symbols and matriarchal concepts, the work attempts to rescue older conceptions of the divine. The resulting series functions as a digital "Birth of Venus" for the AI era, presenting singular, ethereal figures that exist by combining forgotten mythology and emerging technology.

The work is an exploration of the "visual vocabulary" of a machine. By utilizing early-stage text-to-image synthesis, the project reveals the specific textures, light patterns, and archetypal symbols the model associates with god-like creatures. The poetic core of the piece lies in the dialogue between the prompt—often rooted in ancient, earthy matriarchal history—and the output, which is inherently synthetic and ghostly. These singular figures act as a mirror to our own collective unconscious, showing how a model trained on human data chooses to assemble the elements of power, grace, and the ethereal into a cohesive representation of the feminine divine.

## Process

The development of this series focused on iterative prompt engineering: a cyclic process of feeding different information with different sizes and granularities of details, refining textual inputs to see how the AI would prioritize different cultural symbols. Using the first generation of Stable Diffusion, I experimented with the tension between specific descriptors of ancient goddesses and broader, abstract terms for divinity. The process was less about generating a perfect image and more about observing the machine's bias and inventive potential, opening a breach into their dataset. Many of the results were left intentionally raw to highlight the early artifacts of AI generation, emphasizing the birth of a new visual language that is simultaneously futuristic and deeply rooted in our oldest cultural memories.
