## Overview

The Smooth Technology annual party is a staple of the New York creative tech scene, transforming their Brooklyn studio into a showcase of inventive design and experimental engineering. Back in 2024, during their Holiday party at the end of the year, I was tasked with creating a series of visual loops that would set the tone for the evening and draw visitors into the "vibe" of the space from the moment they arrived. The work functions as a digital tribute to the people behind the projects, turning the studio's own crew and architecture into the primary medium. By centering the visuals on the studio’s identity and community, the installation served as both a welcome and a high-energy backdrop for the gathering of builders and artists.

## Process

The creation of these pieces required a fast-paced workflow of photogrammetry and motion graphics.
The idea was that all loops would incorporate the idea of tunnels with a content translating a trippy, kaleidoscopic sensation that played with the viewer's depth perception. I did 3D scans of the entire crew, refining the meshes to maintain a balance between recognizable human forms and abstract digital artifacts, implementing the whole team into one of the tunnel environments. While for the other loops, I played around with the logo and visual effects. The final content was mapped specifically onto the studio’s custom-made triangle LED wall modules, with the loops also illuminating the physical LEDs.
