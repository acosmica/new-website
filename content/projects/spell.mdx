## Overview

<Figure src="/projects/spell/assets/spell-01.jpg" aspect="3/2" />

SPELL started as my Master's thesis at NYU's ITP and it is the culmination of research into the intersection of magic, technology, and nature. The work challenges the "black box" nature of modern tools like generative AI, which are often intentionally obscured to feel incomprehensible to the public. By stepping into the dome, visitors are invited to act as rightful sorcerers, using their own hands to navigate, enchant and influence a reimagined version of nature. This collective and individual reclamation transforms the machine from a distant authority into a collaborative medium for awe and wonder, fostering a deep ritual of reconnection between humans, nature and the digital presence.

## Process

<Pair a="/projects/spell/assets/spell-02.jpg" b="/projects/spell/assets/spell-03.jpeg" aspect="3/2" />

The creation of SPELL involved an extensive research phase into how "magic" is purposefully designed into immersive exhibitions to either distance or engage the viewer. The installation utilizes a Leap Motion controller to track intricate hand movements, translating intangible gestures into real-time variables that drive the generative AI engine. The visuals, a meticulously crafted 360-degree environment, were inspired by the primitive feeling of awe we find in organic patterns and botanical beauty. Through precise prompt engineering, the system renders unique, trippy, and evocative landscapes for every participant.

<Trio a="/projects/spell/assets/spell-21.png" b="/projects/spell/assets/spell-22.png" c="/projects/spell/assets/spell-23.png" aspect="1/1" />

Because the visuals are synthesized in real-time based on the specific combination of movement and machine randomness, no two experiences are identical. The result is a fleeting, ephemeral encounter where the participant holds the power of creation literally in their palms.

<Trio a="/projects/spell/assets/spell-31.png" b="/projects/spell/assets/spell-32.png" c="/projects/spell/assets/spell-33.png" aspect="1/1" />

The project started in a smaller setting and scaled to a massive 40-foot dome size for international festivals. This required optimizing the latency between the gesture-tracking hardware and the AI rendering pipeline to ensure the "magic" felt instantaneous and responsive. I spent months refining the prompt architecture to achieve a visual language that felt both alien and familiar, balancing the latest generative models with a deeply grounded, nature-inspired aesthetic.
