## Overview

"Vai de Game" comprised two distinct digital activations designed to transform the VaideBet brand space into a high-energy entertainment hub. The primary installation was a competitive, touchless multiplayer game that allowed visitors to duel in teams, set within a physically immersive environment enhanced by projection mapping. To complement this, a virtual "scratch card" mini-game was developed to provide quick, ludic entertainment for guests in high-traffic areas. Together, these experiences connected traditional brand promotion and interactive play, centering the festival-goers as the protagonists and engaging new users for their platform.

<Figure src="/projects/vaidesorte/assets/sorte-04.png" aspect="9/5" />

The main game featured an aesthetic inspired by the early days of Atari, juxtaposing retro-digital visuals with cutting-edge interaction. It utilized high-precision depth cameras with hand-tracking and gesture detection, allowing players to control the action entirely through physical movement without touching a single surface.

<Pair a="/projects/vaidesorte/assets/sorte-01.png" b="/projects/vaidesorte/assets/sorte-02.png" aspect="16/9" />

The virtual scratch card mini game reimagined a classic analog game of chance for the mobile era. Developed as a custom app, it prompted players to reveal hidden golden coins featuring the brand logo, turning a mundane wait in a queue into a moment of potential reward and playful engagement.

<Figure src="/projects/vaidesorte/assets/sorte-03.jpg" aspect="3/2" />

## Process

As one of the Creative Directors at thecode, I co-directed both installations from conceptualization through to technical completion. I oversaw all the pipeline from programming to the spatial design and graphic material.

<Figure src="/projects/vaidesorte/assets/sorte-05.png" aspect="9/5" />

The challenge for the main game lay in the environmental design; I coordinated the integration of projection mapping with the game engine to ensure the scenography felt responsive to the digital duel. Calibrating the gesture detection for a loud, crowded festival environment was a rigorous technical process, requiring low-latency feedback to ensure the competitive duel felt fair and instinctive.

For the scratch card app, thecode developed everything from the 3d assets to the app shipping, with a big focus on UI/UX simplicity and visual delight, ensuring the mechanics were accessible enough to be played instantly by any guests while they navigated the festival grounds.
